History

The Rubik’s Cube was invented in 1974 by Ernő Rubik, a Hungarian architect and professor. It began as a teaching tool to explain 3D spatial relationships. In 1980, it launched globally and quickly became a worldwide craze. Since then, new methods, competitions, and variations have kept the cube popular across generations.

Over time, speedsolving techniques like CFOP, Roux, and ZZ emerged. Hardware also improved with magnets, better corner cutting, and smoother mechanisms, helping solvers achieve astonishing times.

World Cube Association (WCA)

The WCA governs official speedcubing competitions globally. It sets rules, recognizes events, tracks results, and maintains records. Competitions follow standardized regulations to ensure fairness and comparability across countries.
